The Panji Stories

A uniquely SEA heritage

Panji is a Javanese tale that was originally written (and set) in 11th-13th Centuries – a time when the Kingdom of Srivijaya, and subsequently Majapahit, dominated the southern part of Southeast Asia (Indonesian Archipelago & Malay Peninsula).

Due to its universal appeal, Panji stories have been adapted many times across the centuries by different countries in Southeast Asia. The stories are popular in Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Cambodia, and Myanmar. Panji Tales Manuscripts have become a UNESCO Registered Heritage in 2017.

This 360imx project aims to adapt the Panji stories for interactive and immersive experience. The story that we are adapting for the 360imx is based on a version told in “Hikayat Panji Semirang”, which is one of the more modern retellings of the Panji story. The story is chronicled in Bahasa Melayu, and can be found in a few books at NUS Libraries’ Singapore-Malaysia Collection.
